Finding Political Voice

Filipino Communities Engagement in American Politics

Research Question

What helps Filipino immigrants and second generation Filipino Americans those specifically living in a culturally rooted household overcome cultural and structural barriers to engage confidently in American politics?

Why Does This Matter?

This topic is important because Filipino Americans are one of the largest Asian American communities in the United States, yet they continue to be underrepresented in political participation and leadership. Many Filipino immigrants and their families face challenges such as language barriers, unfamiliar political systems, long work hours, and fear of making mistakes. Cultural values can also influence how comfortable individuals feel speaking up or getting involved. When communities are disengaged from politics, their needs and concerns may not be fully addressed. By understanding what helps Filipino and Filipino American communities overcome these barriers, this research promotes inclusion, stronger representation, and a more equitable democracy.

About Me

Hi! My name is Czarina!

Growing up in a Filipino household, I saw firsthand how difficult it was for adults in my family to participate in American politics. My parents worked long hours and often felt confused by voting forms and election information, especially because English is not their first language. Political conversations were rare in our home because there was fear of saying the wrong thing or disrespecting the authority. In contrast, I learned about the U.S. government in school but struggled to translate that knowledge into action at home. Watching this disconnect made me realize that political participation is not just about wanting to vote. It's about access, confidence, education, and support. This personal experience inspired me to explore Filipino families and navigate cultural expectations while trying to engage in American democracy.
